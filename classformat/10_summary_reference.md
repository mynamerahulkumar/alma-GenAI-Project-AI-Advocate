Summary
What did we learn?
Access control regulates and controls access to resources based on defined policies and rules.
It ensures that users or entities can only access the resources they are authorized to use.
Access control mechanisms help protect sensitive information, maintain the integrity of systems, and prevent unauthorized disclosure or modification.
Authorization determines the actions or operations that authenticated users are allowed to perform within a system.
It grants appropriate permissions and privileges based on user roles, responsibilities, or attributes.
By enforcing authorization, organizations can control access to sensitive functions and data, preventing unauthorized actions.
Authentication is the process of verifying the identity of users or entities.
It ensures that only authorized individuals can access a system or resource.
Different authentication mechanisms, such as passwords, biometrics, or multifactor authentication, can be used to verify identities.
